Conversion FunnelObservation: Comparing the Conversion Rate - The shopping_cart to purchase conversion rate is higher than the expected decline in conversion rate in the sequence. This means customers are going straight to purchase more often than comparing their items in the shopping cart. The Total Conversion Rate indicates only a third of customers accessing the website is actually adding items into their shopping cart for purchase, and only a third of that is actually purchasing it. Pairing these two rates together shows signs of limited activity of customers "window shopping" or lower basket size per customer.
6
Retention RatesObservation: The retention rate per cohort drops dramatically between the age 0 to age 1, going into the single digits % over the span of 5 months. Notably, there was slightly more interest in the initial month following September of 2020, but has since plateaued at 6% retention at the 1 month period. There was also a max initial peak of 238 unique user_ids in November of 2020 during the cohort's first month, but has since been on a gradual decline in activity. Overall, the analysis shows retention primarily within the first month of activity and then there's an extreme drop off of customer retention after that consistently in the past 5 months. This indicates an area of opportunity for the company to review and evaluate how the website is functioning and being advertised towards customer loyalty to the e-commerce platform.

AnalysisDescription
10
Raw DataThis is the initial dataset / raw transaction logs from the e-commerce company. Refer below for meta-data. The timespan ranges from 2020-09-24 to 2021-02-28 - a 5 month period. It includes event types - view, shopping cart, purchase, tracking customer movement across the e-commerce platform. For the purpose of this analysis, we used the event types, unique count of user_id, and event_date to create the conversion funnel and cohort analysis.
11
Conversion FunnelThis is an analysis of how well the website is converting product page views into purchases. Total conversion rate is the ratio of users (a number of unique user_id) at a particular stage to the number of users at the first stage - the stages being the event types: viewing, to shopping cart to purchase. Conversion Rate is the ratio of users at a particular stage to the number of users at the previous stage. Both are shown as percentages. Note: Analysis is based on the overall picture of all products of the e-commerce platform, not to the specific brands or category code, which would be very helpful for further analysis comparing individual product sales performance or category sales.
12
Retention RatesAn analysis of the retention rate telling how many users in a cohort have remained active compared to the initial number of users. This assessment allows the business to know how long a customer has used the product, which can be a big factor in product revenue. Cohorts were formed based on time periods - month-to-month - and was tracked on a monthly basis per cohort up through the past 5 months. Notably, the most recent month does not have enough data to compare to the previous 4 months of cohorts. Note: Analysis is based on the overall picture of all products of the e-commerce platform, not to the specific brands or category code, which would be very helpful for further analysis comparing individual product sales performance or category sales.
